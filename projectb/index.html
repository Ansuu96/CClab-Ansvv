Before it Fades

This project explores the gradual loss of culture due to Mongolia's small population.

traditions fade and cultural practices become less visible day by day, there is a risk that future generations may lose touch with their own heritage. In this digital era, physical communication objects such as letters, are disappearing, this project is a way of preserving cultural memory. Through the use of stamp cards, it captures key elements of Mongolian identity.

Press A for Harvaa, a traditional Mongolian Archery where archers shoot arrows at small targets during festivals like Naadam.

Press B for Barildaan, traditional Mongolian Wrestling "Bökh".Mongolian wrestling, or Bökh, is a centuries old tradition that embodies strength, technique, and respect.

Press C for Statue of Sukhbaatar, the famous patriot characterizes the square, and the square is named after this historic figure.

Press D for Mongolian Horse Racing, where young riders race across long steppes of Mongolia during Naadam festival.

Press E for Tsam Dance, a sacred masked dance performed in Buddhist ceremonies to represent spirits and protection.

Press R for Tsereg, The People’s Revolution of 1921 (or Mongolian Revolution of 1921) was a military and political event which were helped by the Soviet authorities to rebel against the outside occupation forces in Mongolia.
